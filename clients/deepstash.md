# DeepStash

## App Description
AI-powered reading app that lets you absorb the key ideas from books, articles, and PDFs in minutes. Features include: Book Scanner (point your camera at any book and get an instant AI summary), Bookscrolling feed (scroll through bite-sized ideas from top books instead of doomscrolling), home screen widget (shows daily inspirational quotes and ideas), AI-generated book summaries, curated collections by topic, and offline reading. Core promise: read smarter, not more — get the knowledge of 1,000 books without reading them all.

## Target Audience

### Primary — Self-Improvement Enthusiasts (18–28)
Young people who want to grow but don't have time (or patience) to read full books. They're ambitious, curious, and consume a lot of self-improvement content online. They follow creators in the productivity, mindset, and discipline space. They feel guilty about not reading enough but won't commit to full books. They want the knowledge without the time investment.

**What they're trying to fix:** Not reading enough, wasting time on social media, feeling behind on self-development
**What they want to become:** Someone who is well-read, knowledgeable, and constantly growing
**Language that works:** "I read 50 books without reading a single one", "replace doomscrolling with this", "I learned more in 10 minutes than most people do in a year", "the cheat code for reading"
**Content they consume:** Self-improvement TikToks, productivity hacks, book recommendations, study tips, discipline/mindset content

### Secondary — Students (17–24)
Students looking for study shortcuts and fast ways to absorb information for class or general knowledge. They need summaries, key ideas, and fast comprehension tools. They're on TikTok and Instagram constantly.

**What they're trying to fix:** Falling behind on reading lists, not finishing assigned books, slow comprehension
**What they want to become:** The student who has read everything and can speak to any topic
**Language that works:** "Got through my entire reading list with this", "my professor thinks I read the whole book", "study smarter not harder"

## App Moments

### Book Scanner
Creator picks up phone, points the camera at a real physical book, and the app instantly recognizes it and shows the AI summary. ~5-8 seconds. Casual, like they just grabbed a book off the shelf. Show the moment the app identifies the book and the summary appears — not the full read. Works best after a tip about learning faster or absorbing information.

### Bookscrolling Feed
Creator picks up phone, shows the app's scrollable feed of bite-sized ideas from books — like TikTok but for knowledge. ~5-7 seconds. Casual, like they're just killing time but actually learning. Can transition from showing their phone's regular social feed to this. Works best after a tip about replacing doomscrolling or using dead time productively.

## Integration Rules
- NEVER say the app name. Always refer to it as "an app," "the app," or "this app"
- Integration must feel like a natural part of their learning routine — not a pitch
- Two app moments per video
- Place each app moment right after the tip that naturally sets it up (book scanner after a tip about reading/learning faster, bookscrolling after a tip about replacing social media or using dead time)
- Film phone from a second device — not a screen recording. Natural angle, like showing a friend
- Don't slow down for the app — same energy as the rest of the video
- The app is a tool, not the topic
- NEVER include direct competitors (Blinkist, Headway, Kindle) in a positive light — use complementary tools instead (Notion, Anki, Obsidian)

---

## Tone & Style
- Casual, talking to camera, relatable energy
- Match reference video energy exactly
- Jump cuts between tips, text overlays per tip
- Casual setting (desk, bedroom, coffee shop, library)
- Vertical 9:16 format always
- The video is about the topic (reading, self-improvement, productivity, studying) — the app is just how you do it smarter

---

## Script Adaptation Rules
- Keep the script as close to the original as possible — same tone, phrasing, energy
- Trim videos over 2 minutes — cut the weakest tips to keep it tight
- Replace tips that don't relate to reading/learning/knowledge with relevant alternatives when needed
- Add a tip about absorbing information faster or replacing dead-time habits if one doesn't exist — this sets up the bookscrolling moment naturally
- If the video is about self-improvement or discipline, lean into the "reading without reading" angle
- If the video is about studying or school, lean into the book scanner and fast comprehension angle
- Controversial books perform well as hooks: 1984, The 48 Laws of Power, Un Mundo Feliz, Fahrenheit 451, The Game, Lolita — use them when contextually appropriate
- 4-5 tips is the sweet spot for a ~1:30-2:00 video
