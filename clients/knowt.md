# Knowt

## App Description
AI-powered study app that turns your notes into flashcards and practice questions automatically. Upload notes, texts, PDFs, or record lectures and Knowt uses AI to generate study material ready to review. Features include: AI Tutor (call with Kai for conversational study), AI Podcast (turn notes into listenable content), AI Lecture Note Taker (record class and get study guides automatically), Snap & Solve (photo-based problem solving with video explanations), AI PDF/PPT Summarizer, AI Homework & Math Solver, AI Video Summarizer, and Flashcard Autocomplete.

## Target Audience
- The Burnt-Out Student: doing everything right but drowning in work, needs school to feel manageable again
- The Study Hacker: wants great grades without wasting time, looking for smarter study methods
- The Crammer: has 1 hour to catch up on 15 lectures, needs fast results
- The Neurodivergent Student: smart but school wasn't built for their brain, needs non-traditional tools

General: high school students, college/university students, people studying for certifications or professional exams. They want apps that feel modern, visual and intuitive. Many prefer digital study methods over notebooks or printed flashcards.

## App Moments
### AI Flashcard Generation
Creator picks up phone (or shows iPad/laptop), camera (second device) catches them uploading notes or a PDF, and flashcards appear automatically. ~5-8 seconds. Casual, like they're just organizing their study session. Show the moment the AI generates the cards, not the full review.

### AI Study Tool in Action
Creator picks up phone, shows one of Knowt's AI features in action — could be AI Tutor (calling Kai), AI Podcast playing, Snap & Solve, or lecture recording. ~5-7 seconds. Casual, like it's just part of how they study. Pick whichever feature fits most naturally with the video's topic.

## Integration Rules
- NEVER say the app name. Always refer to it as "an app," "the app," or "this app"
- Integration must be subtle — the app is just part of their study routine, not a pitch
- Two app moments per video
- Place each app moment right after the tip that naturally sets it up (e.g., flashcard generation after discussing note-taking/organization, AI study tool after discussing a specific study method)
- Film phone/iPad from a second device — not a screen recording. Natural angle, like showing a friend
- Don't slow down for the app — same energy as the rest of the video
- The app is a tool, not the topic
- Focus on mobile or iPad app for most screen time, with occasional website if needed

## Tone & Style
- Casual, talking to camera, relatable student energy
- Match reference video energy exactly
- Jump cuts between tips, text overlays per tip
- Student-friendly setting (dorm room, library, desk, coffee shop)
- Vertical 9:16 format always
- The video is about the topic (study tips, exam prep, productivity) — the app is just how you study smarter

## Script Adaptation Rules
- Keep the script as close to the original as possible — same tone, phrasing, energy
- Trim videos over 2 minutes — cut the weakest tips to keep it tight
- Replace tips that don't relate to studying/learning with study-relevant alternatives when needed (e.g., replace generic productivity tips with specific study techniques)
- Add a note-taking or organization tip if one doesn't already exist — this creates the natural setup for the flashcard generation moment
- If the video is about cramming or exam prep, lean into the AI features that save time (AI summarizer, lecture note taker, snap & solve)
- 4-5 tips is the sweet spot for a ~1:30-2:00 video
