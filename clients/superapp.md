# Superapp

## App Description
No-code platform that lets anyone create native iOS applications and launch them to the App Store in days, not months. Unlike web-wrapper solutions (Bubble, Glide, Adalo), Superapp generates true native Swift code that passes Apple's review process. Features include: drag-and-drop visual builder, native Swift code generation, full code ownership and export, App Store compliance built-in, and rapid iteration. Users go from idea to App Store submission without writing a single line of code.

## Target Audience
- Indie Hackers & Solo Founders (25-40): building their first app or MVP, need to validate ideas quickly, active in #buildinpublic community
- Non-Technical Founders: business professionals with app ideas but no coding skills or technical co-founder
- Product Designers & UX Professionals (24-38): want to bring designs to life without learning to code
- Serial Entrepreneurs: launching multiple projects, need to move fast and test ideas efficiently
- Small Business Owners: looking to create custom iOS apps for their customers

General: tech-savvy but not necessarily technical, ages 25-45, active on X/Twitter and YouTube, value speed, efficiency and autonomy. Frustrated by expensive developers ($80K-$150K/year), long learning curves (6-12 months to learn Swift), or web wrapper tools that get rejected from the App Store.

## App Moments
### Visual Builder in Action
Creator picks up phone or shows laptop screen, camera (second device) catches them dragging and dropping elements to build an app screen. ~5-8 seconds. Casual, like they're just working on their project. Show the moment of building — adding a button, designing a screen, connecting navigation. Fast and fluid, not a tutorial.

### App Store Submission / Live App
Creator shows the finished app running natively on their iPhone, or shows the App Store submission/approval screen. ~5-7 seconds. Casual, like a proud moment — "look what I just shipped." Can show the app on their phone or the "Ready for Sale" status in App Store Connect.

## Integration Rules
- NEVER say the app name. Always refer to it as "a platform," "this tool," or "a no-code tool"
- Integration must be subtle — the tool is just part of their workflow, not a pitch
- Two app moments per video
- Place each app moment right after the tip that naturally sets it up (e.g., visual builder after discussing building/creating something, App Store moment after discussing launching or shipping)
- Film screen from a second device — not a screen recording. Natural angle, like showing a friend what you're working on
- Don't slow down for the app — same energy as the rest of the video
- The tool is how they execute, not the topic of the video
- Emphasize speed and simplicity — the creator should look like they're breezing through it

## Tone & Style
- Confident, direct to camera, entrepreneurial energy
- Match reference video energy exactly
- Jump cuts between tips, text overlays per tip
- Workspace/home office/coffee shop setting
- Vertical 9:16 format always
- The video is about the topic (launching an app, entrepreneurship, side projects, building in public) — the tool is just how they make it happen

## Script Adaptation Rules
- Keep the script as close to the original as possible — same tone, phrasing, energy
- Trim videos over 2 minutes — cut the weakest tips to keep it tight
- Replace tips that don't relate to building/launching/entrepreneurship with relevant alternatives when needed
- Add a "just ship it" or "stop overthinking, start building" tip if one doesn't already exist — this creates the natural setup for the visual builder or App Store moment
- If the video is about side hustles or making money, lean into the speed and cost savings angle (shipped in days, didn't spend $50K on developers)
- 4-5 tips is the sweet spot for a ~1:30-2:00 video
- The narrative should always support the idea that building an app is now accessible to everyone
