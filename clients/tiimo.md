# Tiimo

## App Description
Visual AI-powered daily planner that helps people organize their real life without feeling overwhelmed. Built originally for neurodivergent individuals (ADHD, Autism), now serves anyone who struggles with traditional productivity tools. Won App of the Year for iPhone 2025 at the App Store Awards. 500,000+ active users worldwide. Features include: visual day planning (bubble or timeline view), AI Co-Planner (brain dump → structured schedule), to-do list with AI prioritization, task celebration (dopamine-boosting completion visualization), focus timer for time agnosia, widgets & live activities (home screen/lock screen/Apple Watch), mood tracking, and multi-platform sync (iPhone, iPad, Mac, Web, Apple Watch). Free to download with a subscription (7-day free trial) that unlocks additional features.

## Target Audience
- The Juggling Professional (30-40): urban professional, mentally stretched by work/relationships/kids, capable but cognitively overloaded, small tasks slip through the cracks
- The Overcommitting ADHDer (30-40): adult with ADHD navigating career and social life, struggles to prioritize under pressure, tends to overcommit and edge toward burnout
- The Productivity Hunter: neurotypical professional motivated by progress and efficiency, systems-oriented, drawn to credibility and proven performance, wants a competitive edge

General: ages 25-40 (core 28-38), US/UK/Nordics/Australia, heavy TikTok/Instagram Reels consumers, active in ADHD/productivity/wellness communities. Values mental health, self-awareness, aesthetic design, and non-judgmental tools.

## App Moments
### AI Co-Planner Brain Dump
Creator picks up phone, camera (second device) catches them speaking or typing a messy brain dump into the AI Co-Planner — random thoughts, tasks, appointments all jumbled together. Then the AI organizes it into a clean visual schedule. ~5-8 seconds. Casual, like they're just getting their thoughts out. Show the moment the AI transforms chaos into a structured plan.

### Visual Day Timeline
Creator picks up phone, shows their visual day plan — the colorful bubble or timeline view with their tasks laid out. ~5-7 seconds. Casual, like glancing at their plan before starting the next thing. Can also show widgets on home screen, lock screen, or Apple Watch. The point is: everything is visible, nothing is forgotten.

## Integration Rules
- NEVER say the app name. Always refer to it as "an app," "the app," "this planner," or "this tool"
- Integration must be subtle — the app is just part of their daily routine, not a pitch
- Two app moments per video
- Place each app moment right after the tip that naturally sets it up (e.g., brain dump after discussing feeling overwhelmed/scattered, visual timeline after discussing staying on track/not forgetting things)
- Film phone from a second device — not a screen recording. Natural angle, like showing a friend
- Don't slow down for the app — same energy as the rest of the video
- The app is a tool, not the topic
- NEVER use the phrase "time blindness" — always use "time agnosia"
- NEVER refer to neurodivergence as a "superpower"
- NEVER use "high functioning" or "low functioning"
- AVOID obvious AI-generated phrasing (e.g., "Turn chaos into clarity", "You're not lazy, just overwhelmed")
- Include one clear "life benefit" per video (which daily life challenge does this help with?)
- Mention that the app is free to download when contextually appropriate

## Tone & Style
- Warm, relatable, slightly vulnerable but empowered
- Match reference video energy exactly
- Jump cuts between tips, text overlays per tip
- Cozy/real settings (home office, bedroom desk, kitchen table, couch)
- Vertical 9:16 format always
- The video is about the topic (productivity, ADHD life, daily routines, mental health) — the app is just how they stay grounded
- Aesthetic and intentional — matches Tiimo's visual, colorful brand

## Script Adaptation Rules
- Keep the script as close to the original as possible — same tone, phrasing, energy
- Trim videos over 2 minutes — cut the weakest tips to keep it tight
- Replace tips that don't relate to planning/organization/mental clarity with relevant alternatives when needed
- Add a "brain dump" or "getting thoughts out of your head" tip if one doesn't already exist — this creates the natural setup for the AI Co-Planner moment
- If the video is about ADHD or neurodivergence, lean into the executive function support angle (the app does the organizing so your brain doesn't have to)
- If the video is about general productivity, lean into the visual planning and AI prioritization angle
- 4-5 tips is the sweet spot for a ~1:30-2:00 video
- Can reference that the app won App of the Year 2025 if it fits naturally (e.g., "I found this app that literally won App of the Year")
